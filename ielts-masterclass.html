Flaw 1 — No Idea Extension

Every sentence is an unsupported claim. "I think technology is not good" — and then it stops. There is no reason, no example, no consequence, no contrast. A Band 8 answer would follow every opinion with at least one of: a reason, a real-world example, a contrast, or a projected outcome. This answer gives the examiner nothing to reward beyond the initial assertion.

Flaw 2 — Weak Lexical Resource

Vocabulary is drawn entirely from the 2,000 most common English words. The most specific terms used are "convenient" and "deep." The candidate entirely misses opportunities to use topic-specific language: mediated communication, social presence, non-verbal cues, digital literacy, interpersonal disconnect, asynchronous interaction. In IELTS terms, there is zero evidence of "less common lexical items" — a Band 8 requirement.

Flaw 3 — Narrow Personal Frame

The entire answer is anchored in "my friends and me" and "I think." There is zero evidence the candidate can discuss the issue at a societal level. A Part 3 question explicitly requires moving beyond personal experience into abstract, analytical discussion. The candidate never zooms out to trends, demographics, cultural shifts, or generational differences.

Understand the 8.0 Architecture

The Band 8.0 Thinking Pattern

1

Direct Answer with Nuance

Instead of "technology is not good," the 8.0 candidate says: "I partly agree with that proposition, though I think the reality is more nuanced. Technology has undoubtedly altered how we interact, but whether that represents a decline in quality depends on which dimension of communication we examine."

2

Claim → Reason → Example → Link Back

Every point follows this chain. For example: CLAIM — "Instant messaging has reduced the depth of spontaneous conversation." REASON — "Because text-based exchanges lack non-verbal cues that carry so much meaning." EXAMPLE — "A friend might type 'I'm fine' when distressed, but without hearing their voice, the subtle tremor that would betray their real emotional state is absent." LINK BACK — "So while we communicate more frequently, the quality of each exchange may have diminished."

3

Abstract Generalisation

After making personal observations, zoom out to societal trends: "There is a broader societal trend toward what sociologists call 'ambient awareness' — where continuous, low-effort digital contact replaces deeper, episodic face-to-face engagement." Use impersonal structures: "it could be argued that," "there is a tendency for," "research suggests that."

4

Balanced Conclusion with Forward-Looking Perspective

End with synthesis, not repetition: "Ultimately, I would argue the question is not whether technology has reduced communication quality — it has transformed it. The responsibility lies with individuals and institutions to cultivate digital habits that preserve meaningful human connection."

Surface-Level Connection vs. Genuine Depth

虽然社交媒体让我们能跟更多人保持联系，但这种联系往往是表面化的，缺少真正的深度。

Translate into English. Apply the Claim → Reason → Example pattern. Use topic-specific vocabulary. Avoid "good," "bad," "more people," and "talk."

Band 8.0 Model Answer
While social media platforms enable us to maintain contact with a far wider network of acquaintances than would otherwise be possible, the connections they foster tend to be superficial — consisting largely of curated highlights and brief reactions rather than the sustained, vulnerable dialogue that builds genuine intimacy.
Key upgrades: "enable us to maintain contact" (not "help us talk"), "far wider network" (not "more people"), "curated highlights" (specific term), "sustained, vulnerable dialogue" (precise collocation), "builds genuine intimacy" (not "makes us closer").
Structure: CLAIM (social media enables broad contact) → QUALIFICATION (but it's superficial) → REASON (curated highlights vs. vulnerable dialogue) → CONSEQUENCE (no genuine intimacy).
Exercise 2

Spontaneous vs. Scheduled Interaction

过去人们见面聊天的时机会自然产生，现在一切都得刻意安排，这让社交变得更有压力。

Translate into English. Use abstract nouns, a discourse marker ("paradoxically"), and end with a forward-looking consequence.

Band 8.0 Model Answer
In previous generations, spontaneous social encounters arose organically from the rhythm of daily life — neighbours crossing paths, colleagues sharing lunch, extended families living in proximity. Today, nearly every interaction requires deliberate scheduling, which paradoxically makes socialising feel like an obligation rather than a source of rejuvenation, and may contribute to the rising prevalence of social fatigue that researchers have documented in digitally saturated populations.
Key upgrades: "spontaneous social encounters arose organically" (not "people met naturally"), "the rhythm of daily life" (natural collocation), "paradoxically" (sophisticated discourse marker), "obligation rather than a source of rejuvenation" (contrast pair), "rising prevalence of social fatigue" (topic-specific term), "digitally saturated populations" (not "people who use phones a lot").
Structure: PAST CONTEXT (organic encounters) → PRESENT SHIFT (deliberate scheduling) → PARADOX (obligation, not rejuvenation) → FORWARD-LOOKING (social fatigue trend).
Exercise 3

Neutral Technology + Intentional Use

我觉得技术本身没有好坏，关键在于我们如何使用它。如果我们有意识地用它来补充而非替代面对面交流，它反而能增强人际关系。

Translate into English. Use impersonal structures ("I would contend that"), a conditional clause, and a concrete example to support the argument.

Band 8.0 Model Answer
I would contend that technology is inherently neutral — it is neither beneficial nor detrimental in isolation — and that the critical variable is the intentionality with which we deploy it. When used deliberately as a supplement to, rather than a substitute for, face-to-face interaction, digital tools can actually strengthen interpersonal bonds. For instance, families separated by international borders can now share daily moments through video calls in ways that were simply unavailable to previous generations, maintaining a sense of shared presence that geography would otherwise erode.
Key upgrades: "inherently neutral" (not "not good or bad"), "critical variable" (abstract noun), "intentionality with which we deploy it" (not "how we use it"), "supplement to, rather than a substitute for" (precise contrast), "strengthen interpersonal bonds" (not "help relationships"), "shared presence that geography would otherwise erode" (sophisticated consequence framing).
Structure: THESIS (technology is neutral) → NUANCE (depends on intentionality) → CONDITIONAL (when used as supplement) → EXAMPLE (separated families, video calls) → LINK BACK (preserving presence).
