Birmingham Roots

Born in 1965 in Birmingham, UK — a city electrified by a musical revolution. Privately educated, Matt attended Bishop Vesey's Grammar School, founded in 1523 — one of the oldest and most distinguished schools in Britain. Growing up, he was in the thick of it — crossing paths with Robert Plant (Led Zeppelin), Bev Bevan (ELO), Toyah, UB40, and The Specials.

The Scientist

In 1983, Matt moved to North Wales to study at Bangor University. In 1986, he became the only person in the world to graduate with a BSc in Marine Botany that year. A scholarship led to a PhD in Aquatic Ecology.

When his supervisor fell ill, Matt took charge. He built a research network spanning the Institute of Ecology, Cardiff University, the Forestry Commission, and Welsh Water — collaborating on the landmark Lynne Brianne Project studying airborne pollution in Welsh waterways.

"I was left in charge of my own destiny."

The Entrepreneur

When ecological research funding vanished in the late 1980s, Matt pivoted. In 1987, he turned a failing hospitality operation into a five-figure profit within two years. In 1991, he leased his first bar/restaurant — launching a 20-year career.

Over two decades, Matt ran over 35 bars, restaurants, and hotels. He chaired two business associations, debated industry policy on TV and radio, and in 1999 led a delegation to the European Parliament in Brussels to lobby for British victuallers.

At Whitbread (parent of Costa Coffee), he helped open a new venue that repaid its entire investment in 6 months — outperforming the company's Dubai flagship.

"Trouble-shooter. Turnaround specialist. From failing pub to five-figure profit in two years."

The Educator

In 2010, Matt reinvented himself again — earning his first teaching qualification during lunch breaks. An internship at a Beijing kindergarten led to Chongqing, then to EF Education First in 2012.

At EF, he rose from teacher to Senior Director of Studies (2021), opening three new centres. His initiatives — Beginner Life Clubs, metrical posters, observation feedback forms — went national.

He designed training materials for World University Games volunteers and co-led the Rural English Teacher Training Program with China's National Center for Educational Technology. Twice won EF Star Teacher, once Star Manager.
