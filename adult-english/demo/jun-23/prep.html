Pre-Class Reading: The Art of Professional Introductions

Read carefully · 5 minutes · Take notes

🌐 显示中文翻译

When you meet someone for the first time in a professional setting, you have approximately seven seconds to make a first impression.
当你在职业场合第一次见到某人时，你大约有七秒钟的时间来留下第一印象。

That's it. Seven seconds.
就这些。七秒。

Before you even finish your first sentence, the person in front of you has already formed an opinion — about your confidence, your competence, and whether they want to work with you.
在你还没说完第一句话之前，对方已经对你形成了判断——关于你的自信、你的能力、以及他们是否想与你共事。

This is known as the 7-second rule, and it's why mastering the art of professional introductions is one of the most valuable skills in business English.
这就是所谓的七秒法则，这也是为什么掌握职业自我介绍的艺术是商务英语中最有价值的技能之一。

A strong professional introduction follows a simple three-step structure: Present → Past → Future.
一个有力的职业自我介绍遵循简单的三步结构：现在 → 过去 → 未来。

First, you state who you are right now — your name, your role, your current position. "Good morning, everyone. My name is Sarah Chen. I'm a marketing director at GlobalTech."
第一步，说出你现在的身份——你的名字、职位、当前角色。"大家早上好，我是Sarah Chen，GlobalTech的市场总监。"

This grounds the listener. It gives them the essential facts.
这让听众有了基本认知，给了他们关键信息。

Second, you share your background and experience — where you've been, what you've done, what you've learned. "I've been leading the Asia-Pacific team for three years. Previously, I built a marketing department from scratch at a startup in Shanghai."
第二步，分享你的背景和经验——你去过哪里、做过什么、学到了什么。"过去三年我一直在领导亚太团队，在此之前我在上海一家创业公司从零开始搭建了市场部。"

This establishes credibility.
这建立了你的可信度。

Third, you look forward — why you're here, what you hope to achieve, what comes next. "I'm here today to learn more about B2B marketing trends and to meet potential collaborators. I'm looking forward to the discussions ahead."
第三步，展望未来——你为什么在这里、你想达成什么、接下来会发生什么。"我今天来这里是想了解B2B营销趋势并结识潜在的合作者。我很期待接下来的讨论。"

This shows purpose and creates an opportunity for connection.
这展示了你的目的，创造了连接的机会。

However, cultural differences play a significant role in how introductions are received.
然而，文化差异在自我介绍如何被接受方面起着重要作用。

In many Western business cultures, a firm handshake and direct eye contact convey confidence and trustworthiness.
在许多西方商业文化中，有力的握手和直接的眼神交流传达出自信和可信度。

A weak handshake or avoiding eye contact can be interpreted as nervousness or dishonesty.
软弱无力的握手或回避眼神交流可能被解读为紧张或不诚实。

In East Asian cultures, a slight bow or a gentler handshake may be more appropriate, and sustained eye contact can sometimes feel confrontational.
在东亚文化中，轻微鞠躬或较轻柔的握手可能更合适，持续的眼神交流有时会让人感到压迫。

The key is adaptability — read the room, observe what others are doing, and adjust accordingly.
关键是适应性——观察环境、注意他人在做什么、并相应调整。

Confident body language is universal: stand tall, keep your shoulders back, and avoid glancing at your phone.
自信的肢体语言是通用的：挺直站立、肩膀向后、避免看手机。

There are also common mistakes to avoid.
还有一些常见错误需要避免。

Never begin with "Myself [Name]" — this is a direct translation error from several languages including Hindi, but in English it sounds awkward and ungrammatical.
千万不要用"Myself [名字]"开头——这是包括印地语在内的几种语言的直译错误，但在英语中听起来尴尬且语法不对。

Don't speak too fast — nerves can make you rush, but a measured pace signals control.
不要说得太快——紧张会让你加速，但稳重的节奏表现出掌控力。

Don't recite your entire CV — a professional introduction is not a biography. It's an elevator pitch: 30 seconds that tell someone who you are, why you matter, and why they should care.
不要背诵你整份简历——职业自我介绍不是传记。这是一个电梯演讲：用30秒告诉别人你是谁、你为什么重要、以及他们为什么应该在意。

Keep it concise, keep it relevant, and always end with warmth. "It's a pleasure to meet you" or "Thank you" is far better than trailing off or saying "that's it."
保持简洁、保持相关、永远以温暖结束。"很高兴认识你"或"谢谢"远比声音渐弱或说"就这样"好得多。

Finally, remember that introductions are the beginning of a conversation, not the end of one.
最后，记住自我介绍是对话的开始，而不是结束。

The goal is not to impress — it's to connect. Building rapport is more important than sounding impressive.
目标不是让人印象深刻——而是建立连接。建立融洽关系比听起来厉害更重要。

Ask questions. Show genuine interest in the other person.
提问。对对方表现出真诚的兴趣。

At a networking event, the person who listens well is often remembered more fondly than the person who talks the most.
在社交活动中，善于倾听的人往往比说得最多的人更令人难忘。

A good introduction opens the door. A good conversation walks through it.
一个好的介绍打开了一扇门。一段好的对话走了进去。

Section A: Matching · 配对题 (8 items)

Match each phrase with its correct meaning. 将每个短语与正确的含义配对。

Match each phrase (1–8) with its correct meaning (a–h).

1. "Good morning, everyone"
2. "keep it short and sweet"
3. "I'm looking forward to"
4. "Please feel free to"
5. "I've heard great things about"
6. "Previously, I worked at"
7. "It's a pleasure to meet you"
8. "I'm responsible for"
Meanings to choose from:
a) A formal greeting for a group
b) Keep it brief and concise
c) I am excited about (a future event)
d) You are welcome to...
e) People have told me positive things about...
f) In the past, my job was at...
g) A polite and warm closing statement
h) My duties include...

Section B: Multiple Choice · 选择题 (5 items)

Choose the best answer for each question. 为每道题选择最佳答案。

Choose the best answer based on the reading passage and the pre-class video.

1. According to the reading, how long do you have to make a first impression?

2. What is the correct order of the 3-step introduction formula?

3. Why is body language important in professional introductions?

4. What is a common mistake in professional introductions?

5. Which phrase is best for ending a professional introduction?

Section A: Matching Answers

1. a — "Good morning, everyone" is a formal greeting for a group.

2. b — "keep it short and sweet" means keep it brief and concise (from the video).

3. c — "I'm looking forward to" expresses excitement about a future event.

4. d — "Please feel free to" is a polite way of saying "you are welcome to."

5. e — "I've heard great things about" means people have told you positive things.

6. f — "Previously, I worked at" refers to a past job.

7. g — "It's a pleasure to meet you" is a polite and warm closing statement.

8. h — "I'm responsible for" means "my duties include."

Section B: Multiple Choice Answers

1. b) 7 seconds — The reading explicitly states the 7-second rule: "you have approximately seven seconds to make a first impression."

2. c) Present → Past → Future — First state who you are now (Present), then your background (Past), then why you're here (Future).

3. b) It conveys confidence and approachability — Body language doesn't replace words, but it reinforces your message. A firm handshake, eye contact, and good posture signal confidence across cultures.

4. d) Saying "Myself [Name]" — This is a direct translation error from several languages. In English, say "My name is [Name]" or "I'm [Name]."

5. c) "Thank you." — A warm, professional close. "That's it" and "I'm done" sound abrupt and unconfident. "Bye" is too casual for a professional setting.
