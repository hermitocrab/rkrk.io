Section 2

同义词三步法

A systematic approach to synonym questions · 告别"凭感觉选"

Step 1

忘了粗体词，先读懂这句话

Ignore the bolded word. Read the full sentence first. Understand its overall meaning and intent before you even look at the options. Context is everything — the bolded word is just one piece of the puzzle.

Step 2

锁定词性 → 词性不对的选项直接划掉

Determine the part of speech of the bolded word (noun, verb, adjective, adverb). Eliminate any options that don't match. This often eliminates 1–2 wrong answers immediately.

Step 3

逐一代入原文 → 找"放进去最自然"的那个

Substitute each remaining option into the sentence. The correct answer should feel natural — it preserves the tone, register, and precise meaning of the original. Don't overthink. The natural choice is usually correct.

Tone & Register — why the "closest" word isn't always the right one

Why Tone Matters in Synonym Questions

Two words can mean nearly the same thing but carry completely different emotional weight. Academic English is especially sensitive to register — choosing the wrong tone can make a correct definition feel out of place in context.

Section 5

Part 1a 真题实战

Practice Set · 6 synonym-in-context questions with instant feedback

1. Many people know they OUGHT TO eat healthily, yet still consume junk food.
"Should" matches the advisory tone of "ought to." "Must" is too strong — it implies obligation, not advice. "Could" and "might" are about possibility, not recommendation.
2. The laboratory demanded PRECISION in every measurement.
"Accuracy" = exactness in measurement. "Truth" is about facts vs. lies. "Detail" means specifics, not precision. "Reality" is the opposite of imagination.
3. All administrative PERSONNEL must attend the briefing.
"Staff" = employees in an organization. "Crew" is for ship/plane/film crews. "Team" emphasizes collaboration. "People" is too general.
4. The weather conditions were highly ABNORMAL for this time of year.
"Unusual" = not normal, not typical. "Rare" means few in number, not necessarily abnormal. "Special" can be positive. "Isolated" = alone, disconnected.
5. The experiment, SEEMINGLY simple, required months of preparation.
"Apparently" = appears to be (neutral observation). "Supposedly" carries doubt. "Clearly" and "evidently" are too certain — the sentence implies the appearance was deceiving.
6. Scientists were able to DEDUCE the structure from the data.
"Determine" = figure out through reasoning/analysis. "Conclude" suggests end of a process. "Calculate" is math only. "Assess" = evaluate, not figure out.

Part 3 初体验

Multi-Text Matching Preview · 认识多文本匹配题型

What is Part 3?

Part 3 presents 4 short texts (A–D) on a related theme. You answer questions by identifying which text contains specific information. Each text may be used more than once — or not at all. This section tests your ability to scan and compare multiple sources quickly.

Preview: Two Sample Passages

Passage A

Paper-making was invented in China during the Han Dynasty around 105 CE. The court official Cai Lun is traditionally credited with refining the process, using mulberry bark, hemp, and rags to create a durable writing surface. This innovation transformed record-keeping and communication across the empire, eventually spreading along the Silk Road to the Islamic world and medieval Europe.

Passage B

Modern paper recycling faces significant challenges. While recycling reduces the demand for virgin wood pulp, the process requires substantial water and energy. Contamination from inks, adhesives, and food waste further complicates recycling efforts. Environmental scientists estimate that recycled paper still accounts for less than 60% of total paper production globally.

Q1. Which text discusses a historical invention?
Passage A describes the invention of paper-making during the Han Dynasty (c. 105 CE) — a clear historical invention.
Q2. Which text mentions environmental problems?
Passage B discusses recycling challenges, water/energy use, contamination, and environmental impact — clear environmental concerns.
