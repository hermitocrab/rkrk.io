The Architect

MEET
KEE

I don't teach English.
I dismantle it and rebuild it as architecture.

L9.0 IELTS Listening
R8.5 IELTS Reading
S8.5 IELTS Speaking

Here's the thing about language teaching: most people treat it like a warehouse. Fill the shelves with vocabulary, grammar rules, idioms. Stack higher. Hope something sticks.

I treat it like architecture. Every student is a site. Every lesson is a blueprint. And every cognitive node we build together is load-bearing — designed to hold weight under real-world pressure.

I learned this the hard way. In 2012, I was the interpreter for the Backstreet Boys during their Chengdu tour. That's not a classroom gig. That's 20,000 people, live microphones, zero margin for error. You don't translate words — you translate energy. You become the bridge between two entire emotional worlds, in real time. That's where I learned that language isn't about correctness. It's about resonance.

After that, I went deep into the system. EF. Expert Teacher — the highest rank you can hit. Then Product Specialist and Trainer of Trainers for all of West China. I trained the trainers. I watched hundreds of teachers teach thousands of students. And I saw the same thing, over and over: content without architecture. Information without integration.

So I built something different.
